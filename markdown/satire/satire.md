# Satire 
## Definition 
The definition of satire:  
> A literary genre that uses wit to expose humanity's flaws/vices in an effort to change or reform mankind and human insistutions.   

> The satire also ridicules foolish social customs/beliefs/traditions and exposes the hypocrisy and stupidity of public figures.  

There are two main types of satire  
1. Horatian Satire  
> **Purpose**: Seeks to criticize, rather than attack, immorality or stupidity.   
> In general, is gentler, more sympathetic, and more tolerant of human holly.  
> Unlike juvenanlian satire, it serves to make us laugh at human folly as opposed to holding our failures up for needing.  
> Tends to ridicule human folly in general or type rather than attack specific persons.  
> Tends to produce a smell.  

2. Juvenalian Satire:
> Harsher than Horatian satire because it often attacks and shows contempt for people  
> To address some evil in society through scorn and ridicule.  
> The Juvenalian satirist approaches his work in a more serious manner and uses dignified language to attack erroneous thinking or vice. 
> Juvenalian satire evokes feelings of scorn, shock, and righteous indignation in the mind of reader. 

## Satirical Devices
*There are some devices are commonly used in satire.*  
1. **Allusion**  
> **Definition**: A passing or casual reference to a well-known/historical person, place or thing, either directly or by implication.  
> **Example**: The pig named *Napoleon*.  

2. **Caricature**
> **Definition**: A picture or description, ludicrously exaggerating the defects of persons or things.  
> **Example**: **Mr Burns** from the **The Simpsons**  
> His traits are blown entirely out of proportion - he is incredibly physically frail (to the point of being blown away by a light breeze)  

3. **Connotations**  
> **Defintion**: The associated or secondary meaning of a word or expression, in addition to its primary meaning/"dictionary Definition"  
> **Example**:  
> The Word: "Cozy"  
> Reality: The apartment is so microscopic that you can touch the stove from your bed.  
> 
4. **Denotation**
> **Definition**: the explicit or direct meaning of a word or expression.   
> **Example**: To express my biological mating interest, I have purchased the served, dying reproductive organs of a thorny bush. I also brought a lump of heavily processed cocoa bean paste, which I expect to ingest. 
5. **Humor**  
>**Definition**: attempting to be comical or amusing: something humourous.  
>**Example**: The principal confiscates a student's beanie because hats are a "major distractino to the learning environment" whie completely ignoring the deafening construction jackhammer tearing up the concrete right outside the classroom window.  

6. **HyperBole**  
> **Definition**: extreme exaggeratin  
> **Example**: If I don't get the new iphone 18 right this second, my social life will literally evaporate and I will be forced to wander the earth as an untagged ghost.   

7. **Irony**:  
> **Definition**: the difference between what is stated and what is real meant(Verbal)  
>, or between what is expected and what actually happens (situational)  
>or when auidences know a secret a character in a text do not(Dramatic)  
> **Example**: A billionaire files a private, gas-guzzing jet to a climate change conference just to give a five-minute speech about how middle-class people need to do a better job rinsing out their recycle yogurt containers.  

8. **Juxtaposition:**
> **Defniition**: placing ideas, words, images and/or phases close together or side by side, especially for comparison or contrast.  
> **Example**: An influencer records a serene, higly-edited video about "finding inner peace and letting go of material things", while their overworked, underpaid assistant runs around in the background carrying twelve shopping bags and an iced coffee. 

9. **Malapropism**:
> **Definition**: An act or habit of misusing words ridiculously; the confusion of words that are similar in sound.  
> **Example**: A corrupt town mayor confidently announces on live TV, "I promise to run the city with complete and utter impotence!"  

10. **Paradox**: 
> **Definition**: a statement or proposition that, despite sound reasoning, leads to a conclusion that seems senseless, logically unacceptable, or self contradictory (but it is in fact true). (Logicall unacceptable but true)  
> **Example**: to prove you are ready o be an independent adult in the real world, you must spend the next four years raising your hand to ask permission to use the bathroom. 

11. **Parody**:
> **Definition**: humorous exaggerated imitation, a style which deliberately seeks to ridicule another style.   
> **Example**: A comedy sketch where 14-year-old freshmen show up to their first day of algebra dressed in designer gowns, speaking entirely in dark, brooding riddles about "the darkness with the quadratic equation"  

12. **Personification**:
>**Definition**: giving human characteristics and qualities to inanimate objects.  
> **Example**: The smartphone gazed lovingly at its human, knowing it only had to send three more push notifications to keep the boy completely paralyzed on the couch for rest of the weekend.

13. **Pun**
>**Definition**: the humorous use of a word or phrase so as to emphasize its different meanings, or the use of words that are alike or nearly alike in sound but different in meaning; a play or word
> **Example**: The greedy loggin company promised the public they would finally turn over a new leaf... right before they chopped down the rest of forest. 

14. **Reversal**
> **Definition**: Reversal of the normal order, roles people play  
> **Example**: The student sit calmly at their desks drinking coffee while the teacher nervously fills in a Scantron bubble sheet, sweating profusely because here salary and career depend entirely on whether she remembers the capital of North Dakota. 

15. **Sarcasm** 
> **Definition**: a sharply mocking or contemptuous remark

16. **Stereotype**:
> **Definition**: works by taking a real or imagined trait of an individual to be true of group to which the individual belongs; they express bias and can be unfair and harmful

17. **Symbolism**:
> **Definition**: giving symbolic meanings to objects, events or relationships; using something tangibile (and object) to represent something intangible

18. **Travesty**:
> **Definition**: the treatment of a noble and dignified subject in an inappropriately trivial manner

19. **Understatement**:
> **Definition**: A form of irony in which something is intentionally represented as less that it is.
