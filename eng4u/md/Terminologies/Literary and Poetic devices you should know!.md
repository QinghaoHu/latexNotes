**Flashback**
> writer presents incidents that occurred before the opening episode

**Foreshadowing** 
> Hint is given of something that will happen later in the story 

**Tone**
> author's attitude to characters and plot; affect the reader's reaction (is often very subtle)

**Mood**
> the atmosphere created by writer... the mood could be happy, scary, sad, suspenseful, depressing

**Irony**
Verbal:
> when someone says the opposite of what they feel or mean (can be sarcasm)

Dramatic:
> reader/audience knows more about a situation than the characters

Situational: 
> Involves a situation in which actions have an effect that is opposite from what was intended, so that the outcome is contrary to what was expected

**Allusion**
> a reference to something or someone famous from literature, religion, history, or popular culture 

**Imagery** 
> A group of descriptive words that help paint a picture in the reader's mind, usually involves the senses

**Metaphor**
> comparing one object to another in the most direct way

**Simile** 
> a comparison between two and things using "like" or "as"

**Oxymoron**
> a short phrase that brings together contradictory terms (eg. violent love, bitter sweet)

**Paradox**
> A confusing sounding statement which actually makes sense 

**Personification**
> giving human characteristics to objects or animals

**Alliteration**
> a series of word that being with the same consonant sound or letter 

**Hyperbole**
> An extreme exaggeration for emphasis

**Onomatopoeia**
> when a word sounds like what it's describing 

**Contrast**
> comparing two things in order to show the differences between them; the includes characters, objects, and events, similar to juxtaposition. 

**Foil**
> a character who is presented as a contrast to a second character, highlighting certain qualities or traits of the other character; the foil character is often portrayed as a contrast to the protagonist 

**Pathetic Fallacy**
> When the mood of the play is reflected in the weather or nature (eg. sad = rain)
