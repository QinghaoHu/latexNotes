Quotations are significant when they:
1. **Provide Character Revelation**
	1. Reveals an aspect of the personality of a character
	2. OR **Provide Character Development**: shows a change in the character's personality
2. __Provide Contrast__:
	1. show the difference between characters or simulations 
	2. Or __Establishes Relationships Between Characters__: reveals characters relationships to one another
3. __Advances or Complicates the Plot__:develops the plot; marks a twist in plot
4. __Provides Comic Relief__:the inclusion of a humorous character, scene, or witty dialogue in an otherwise serious work, often to relieve tension
5. __Contains Background Information__: provides information to establish context about a particular topic or aspect of the text
6. __Establishes setting__: identifies the time and place of the story
7. __Reveals Theme__:highlights an important idea that recurs in the text
8. __Develops conflict__:creates tension between characters and focus
9. __Utilizes a Rhetorical/Poetic Devices/Tragic Term including, But Not Limited to, the following
	1. *Displays Irony*
		1. Verbal or Situational irony: shows irony through speech or situation
			1. Dramatic Irony (Only in the speech): the words have a different meaning for the character than the audience; the audience knows more than the character. the audience is able to see a discrepancy between the character's perception and reality. 
	2. *Provides Suspense*:develops a sense of uncertainty as to the outcome or events
	3. *Develops Pathos*: (powerful emotions, usually of pity or fear) or Catharsis (strong emotional experiences that result in relief or cleansing)
	4. Contains Symbolism: uses a concrete object to represent an idea or feeling
