\subsection{Derivative of Exponential Function}
We can derive the derivative of exponential function from the first principle. 
\begin{proposition}
    [Derivative of Exponential Function]
    $$\forall x, b \in \R, \frac{d}{dx} b^x = \ln b \cdot b^x$$
    \begin{proof}
        Given $f(x) = b^x$
        \begin{align*}
            f'(x) &= \lim_{h \to 0}\frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h} \\
            &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{b^{x + h} - b^{x}}{h} \\
            &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{b^x (b^h - 1)}{h} \\
            &= \lim_{h \to 0} b^x \lim_{h \to 0}\left (\frac{b^h - 1}{h} \right ) \\
            &= b^x \lim_{h \to 0}\left (\frac{b^h - 1}{h} \right )
        \end{align*}
        Then, we need to solve for $$\lim_{h \to 0} \left(\frac{b^h - 1}{h}\right)$$

        \begin{align*}
            \lim_{h \to 0} \left(\frac{b^h - 1}{h}\right) &=   \lim_{h \to 0} \left(\frac{e^{h\ln b} - 1}{h}\right) \\
            &=   \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{e^x - 1}{\frac{x}{\ln b}}\right) \\
            &= \ln b   \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{e^x - 1}{x}\right) \\
            &= \ln b \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{e^x}{1}\right) \\ 
            &= \ln b
        \end{align*}
        Therefore:
        \begin{align*}
            f'(x) &= b^x \ln b
        \end{align*}
    \end{proof}
\end{proposition}

However, individuals may realises Secondary School teachers do not approach derivative of exponential function through L'Hospital Law. 
So, let us use high school way to approach derivative of exponential function. 

Through previous steps, one may realises the derivative of exponential function is the exponential function itself vertically stretched or compressed by a dilation factor.
Given 
\begin{gather*}
    f:\R \to \R \\
    \forall b, x \in \R \\ 
    f(x) = b^x
\end{gather*}
\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
    \hline
    b values & f'(x) compare to f(x) will be\\
    \hline
    $0 < b < \tfrac{1}{e}$ & REFLECTED, STRETCHED\\
    \hline
    $\frac{1}{e} < b < 1$ & REFLECTED, COMPRESSED \\
    \hline 
    $1 < b < e$ & COMPRESSED \\
    \hline 
    $b > e$ & STRETCHED \\
    \hline 
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
