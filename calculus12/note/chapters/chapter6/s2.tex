\subsection{The Dot Product}
\subsubsection{Definition}
\begin{definition} [Dot Product]
The \term{dot product} measures how much two vectors point in the same direction.
\end{definition}

Given Geometry vector $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$, the dot product between these two vectors is given by 
$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = \left|\vec{a}\right|\left|\vec{b}\right|\sin \theta$$
$\theta$ refers to the angle between $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$.\\

Given Cartesian vector $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$, the dot product between these two vectors is given by 
$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1b_1 + a_2b_2$$

\subsubsection{Application of the Dot Product}
In physics, the dot product is used to find out the projection of a vector on another vector. 
You can use this to solve for the components of force. 

One of the application of the dot product is the to find the angle between two vectors.
\begin{example}
    Givem Cartesian vectors $\vec{v}$ and $\vec{u}$, find the angle between these two vectors. 

    \textit{Using the formula of the dot product}
    $$\vec{v} \cdot \vec{u}= \left|\vec{u}\right| \left|\vec{v}\right|\cos \theta$$
    and 
    $$\vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} = v_1u_1 + v_2u_2$$
    we put these two formulas together. 
    \begin{align*}
        \cos \theta &= \frac{
            v_1u_1 + v_2u_2
        }{\left|\vec{u}\right|\left|\vec{v}\right|} \\
        \theta &= \cos^{-1} \left(\frac{
            v_1u_1 + v_2u_2
        }{
            \left(\sqrt{{v_1}^2 + {v_2}^2}\right) \left(\sqrt{{u_1}^2 + {u_2}^2}\right)
        } \right )
    \end{align*}
\end{example} 

\textbf{How to find the projection vector of a vector on another vector?}

Assume we have two Cartestian vectors, $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$. The projection of $\vec{v}$ on $\vec{u}$ is defined as 
$proj_{\vec{u}}\vec{v}$.

Let's solve for $proj_{\vec{u}}\vec{v}$. 

In the previous sectoin, we given that 
$$\left|proj_{\vec{u}}\vec{v}\right| = \left|\vec{v}\right|\cos \theta$$

In order to find the projection vector, we need to multiply the magnitude by a unit vector of $\vec{u}$.
\begin{align*}
    proj_{\vec{u}} \vec{v} &= \left|\vec{v}\right| \cos \theta \left(\frac{1}{\left|\vec{u}\right|}\vec{u}\right)\\
    &= \left|\vec{v}\right| \left(
        \frac{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}}{\left|\vec{u}\right| \left|\vec{v}\right|}
    \right) \left(\frac{1}{\left|\vec{u}\right|}\vec{u}\right) \\
    &= \frac{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}}{\left|\vec{u}\right|^2} \vec{u} \\
    &= \frac{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}}{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u}} \vec{u} \\
    &= \frac{
        v_1u_1 + v_2u_2
    }{
        {u_1}^2 + {u_2}^2
    } \vec{u}
\end{align*}

Also, for the magnitude of $proj_{\vec{u}}\vec{v}$, can also be defined by this:

\[
    \left|proj_{\vec{u}}\vec{v}\right| = \left |\frac{v_1u_1 + v_2u_2}{\left|\vec{u}\right|} \right |
\]
