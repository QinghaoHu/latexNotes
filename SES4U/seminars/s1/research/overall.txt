### **1. Introduction: From Myths to Observation**

* **Purpose:** Show how humans first tried to explain the sky.
* **Key points:**

  * Early civilizations (Babylonians, Egyptians, Mayans) explained celestial events with myths and gods.
  * Example: Sun as a chariot (Egyptian Ra) or gods controlling planets (Babylonians).
  * Limitations: purely narrative, no predictive power.

---

### **2. Ancient Greek Astronomy – Geocentric Model**

* **Purpose:** Introduce systematic observation.

* **Key scientists and contributions:**

  1. **Aristotle (384–322 BCE)**

     * Earth-centered universe (geocentric).
     * Universe is spherical; Earth at center; heavenly bodies move in perfect circles.
  2. **Eudoxus (c. 408–355 BCE)**

     * Developed **concentric spheres model** to explain planetary motion.
  3. **Ptolemy (c. 100–170 CE)**

     * Wrote the *Almagest*.
     * Introduced **epicycles** (small circles on planetary paths) to match observations.
     * This model dominated for 1400+ years.

* **Key point to emphasize:** Greek model was systematic, mathematically descriptive, but **Earth-centered and increasingly complex**.

---

### **3. The Shift to Heliocentric Model**

* **Purpose:** Show how science evolved via observation and theory.

* **Key scientists:**

  1. **Nicolaus Copernicus (1473–1543)**

     * Proposed **Sun-centered (heliocentric) model**.
     * Planets orbit Sun in circular paths.
     * Simplified planetary motion but still used some epicycles.
  2. **Tycho Brahe (1546–1601)**

     * Made **extremely precise astronomical observations**.
     * Tried a hybrid model (Earth center, planets around Sun) but data critical for future discoveries.
  3. **Johannes Kepler (1571–1630)**

     * Used Brahe’s data.
     * **Kepler’s Laws:** planets move in **elliptical orbits**, not circles.
     * Provided strong mathematical support for heliocentric model.
  4. **Galileo Galilei (1564–1642)**

     * Used **telescope to make observations**: Jupiter’s moons, phases of Venus.
     * Provided **evidence supporting heliocentric model**.
  5. **Isaac Newton (1643–1727)**

     * **Universal gravitation** explained planetary motion.
     * Connected physics with astronomy; heliocentric model fully supported.

* **Key point to emphasize:** Each scientist **built on the previous**, moving from ideas to **evidence-based science**.

---

### **4. Conclusion**

* **Key takeaways:**

  * Scientific progress is cumulative and often slow.
  * Observation + theory = modern astronomy.
  * Myth → Geocentric → Heliocentric is **a story of evidence overcoming tradition**.
