\section{Frame of Reference}
\subsection{1 Dimension Frame of Reference}

\begin{definition}
    [Frame of Reference] The motion of objects is measured relative to a perspective or location that we consider to be motionless. 
    This perspective, from which an object's motion is measured, is called a frame of reference. 
\end{definition}

For certain \textcolor{red}{chase and collision} questions, you can use a \textcolor{blue}{frame of reference} 
to solve them.
