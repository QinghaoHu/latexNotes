\section{Equations of Motion}
To start, there are five equations used in constant-acceleration motion.
\[
    \vec{v_{f}} = \vec{v_{i}} + \vec{a}*\Delta t
\]
\[
    \Delta\vec{d} = \frac{1}{2}(\vec{v_{i}} + \vec{v_{f}}) * \Delta t
\]
\[
    \Delta\vec{d} = \vec{v_{i}}\Delta t + \frac{1}{2}\vec{a} * \Delta t^2
\]
\[
    \Delta\vec{d} = \vec{v_{f}}\Delta t - \frac{1}{2}\vec{a} * \Delta t^2
\]
\[
    \vec{v_{f}}^2 = \vec{v_{i}}^2 + 2 \vec{a} \Delta \vec{d} 
\]

\subsection{Format requirements for answering motion questions}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Always include a diagram with every known quantity from the question.
    \item Write $\vec{v}$ to at least two decimal places.
    \item Always list the steps in your answer.
    \item When using a quadratic solver on a calculator, write \textit{*Using Quadratic Eq} in your answer.
    \item When you form a system of equations, label them $1, 2, 3,$ and so on.
    \item Follow the significant-figure rules.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item[!] For \textbf{addition} and \textbf{subtraction}, keep the least number of \textbf{decimal places}.
        \item[!] For \textbf{multiplication} and \textbf{division}, keep the least number of \textbf{significant figures}.
    \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}
